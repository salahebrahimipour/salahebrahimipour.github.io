A brief introduction

I have studied and trained as an art-historian archaeologist during my B.A. and M.A. focusing on Iranian Archaeology. Currently, my PhD studies rely on a combination of field evidence and artefacts studied through computational methods in archaeology, remote sensing and GIS analysis.

My area of interest primarily lies in new archaeological methods, focusing on human interaction between western, northwestern Iran and northern Mesopotamia from the Early Bronze Age to the end of the first millennium BCE. I am particularly interested in socio-ecological dynamics and human interactions, which I have studied through statistical, computational, digital, and GIS methods in archaeology.
